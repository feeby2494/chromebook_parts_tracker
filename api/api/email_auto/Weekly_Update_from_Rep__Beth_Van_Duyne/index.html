Washington, D.C. – On Tuesday, President Biden delivered a discouraging State of the Union Address, reminding us not only that we are losing on the world stage, but that the state of our union is far from normal. After more than a year of navigating from crisis to crisis, Biden failed to acknowledge his role in the disasters plaguing our country or offer any actionable solutions. Without a change in his policies, we will remain stuck in the crises he has made, harming American families and weakening us further on the world stage.

This week, I participated in a House Small Business Committee hearing on leveling the playing field for small businesses. Our entrepreneurs don’t want more handouts from the government – they want a fair opportunity to start and grow their business without government interference.

I also participated in an important Transportation and Infrastructure Committee markup on The Coast Guard Authorization Act of 2022. This bill is a two-year authorization of the Coast Guard’s policies and programs. The bill provides support that enhances Coast Guard operations as we face multiple crises currently occurring in the United States and abroad. It will also provide resources to help more effectively carry out their vital missions of ensuring maritime safety and commerce, stopping the flow of illegal drugs and migrants into the country, enforcing U.S. laws at sea, and protecting our nation’s border.

On Thursday, I spoke at the Airports Council International and American Association of Airport Executives leadership forum. I had the opportunity to highlight the work being done for our airline industry, especially my Unruly Passenger Restraint Review Act and my recent lawsuit against the CDC regarding masks in airports and on commercial planes.
I was glad to meet with the National Kidney Foundation to discuss relevant legislation, funding, and critical research programs for kidney disease. It is important to hear directly from healthcare stakeholders to understand the appropriate policies Congress can pass to improve the health of all Americans.
Our TX-24 Hometown Hero of the week is Donna Cranston, founder of Defenders of Freedom, where she partners with the medical community to support veterans needing brain treatment. Thank you, Donna, for supporting our veterans and impacting their lives for the better.
